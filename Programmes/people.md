### Programmes comm
Tbh, 4-5 people for the planning stage is sufficient IF everyone is helping, even 2-3 REALLY efficient and solid comm members would be enough, but extra manpower is most appreciated on d-day (see ‚ÄúCommitment‚Äù below)  
**Division of labour**
- I didn‚Äôt really divide up the labour or make people‚Äôs roles clear enough (TBH i myself wasn‚Äôt so clear on why/how the previous comm split their roles that way) but here‚Äôs how I would do it if I had to do it again (feel free not to use):
- Games: Should be thinking about - is the game fun? Is it feasible? Does it include enough members of the group?
- Activities: does the activity/game fit with the overall theme/flow/purpose of Ori? What other activities can be incorporated into ori? Can consider liaising with CM/Fundraising
- Proposals: My proposals ICs were the people I trusted the most to be organised and get work done‚Ä¶ Ideally you can trust all members of your comm to do that tho

### GMs
You cannot run Ori without GMs. A good GM can make or break a game. Other than Progs comm (who mostly became GMs anyway) GMs are the best source of feedback on games.
- OGL Bonding Day: I wanted the GMs and the OGLs to play the games together to bond so hopefully they would work better together but it turned out kind of a mess (see ‚ÄúKey pitfalls: OGL Bonding Day‚Äù below) either have the GMs bond with each other or practice running games; don‚Äôt try to do both 
- Can consider doing GM recruitment earlier than OGLs and keeping it open for a longer duration (generally more people want to be OGLs instead)
- Make manpower allocation plans in advance so you can communicate info to GMs (When people feel more involved they‚Äôre usually more invested) (can ask Welfare to help out with this)
- GMs need to be aware of the hard NOs when it comes to game rules etc. but generally they can be quite flexible and change things up for safety reasons/so people have more fun etc.
- Make sure GMs are CLEAR and AWARE of your rules, points system, and the timetable (this is where having a section IC 
- GMs generally tend to be less extroverted/‚Äùonz‚Äù compared to OGLs 
- GMs cannot control 20+ 40+ people on their own, OGLs need to help out as well in terms of getting the OGMs to listen, briefing on rules, etc. (coordinate with Welfare/talk to OGLs yourself to make this clear)
- Where possible I assigned more than 1 GM but ideally they can hold their own, if really not enough manpower after pulling from seniors + other comms + everyone and anyone you know can consider letting OGLs direct some games for their OGs
- There may be conflict between GMs and OGLs, personally I thought it was good that GMs were under Progs and OGLs under Welfare because then any GM-OGL conflict comes down to a discussion between Ocomm directors + each side feels equally represented but maybe its not that deep ü§∑

- Refresher: Last chance to refresh everyone‚Äôs memories before actual Ori
